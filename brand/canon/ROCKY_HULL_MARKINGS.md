# The Two Layers

## Consumer layer — visible immediately
- The RocketPlate logo language (cream hull, navy, Rocket Orange)
- The orange porthole ring
- MO in the porthole

## Discovery layer — found after looking closer
- `GOROCKETPLATE.COM` engraved around the porthole ring
- `MØ 3.3` chest badge (per MO_CHARACTER_BIBLE.md)
- `RP-01` vehicle designation
- Tiny inspection marks · small panel numbers · maintenance labels ·
  serial numbers · `EST.2026`

This is what makes Pixar props and Apple hardware rewarding: the first
impression is clean and friendly; the closer you look, the more
craftsmanship you discover.

# The Mission Ring ⭐ (primary website branding)

`GOROCKETPLATE.COM` — tiny engraved lettering following the circumference
of the orange porthole ring.

- **Laser-engraved into the orange ring. Tone-on-tone. Almost invisible
  until light hits it.**
- Reference feel: Leica lens barrels · Canon L-series rings · a Rolex
  bezel · NASA hatch markings.
- Letter-spacing generous and even; type follows the ring's curve
  perfectly; no plaque, no contrast panel, no glow.
- It reads as engineering, not advertising. Someone may miss it on first
  visit — when they find it, it feels like a rewarding detail, never an ad.

# Production law

1. **Real typography only.** Engravings are exact, spell-checked strings —
   never AI pseudo-glyphs. If a generation cannot render a marking cleanly
   and exactly, ship the surface clean and apply the marking in post
   (vector/compositing). A misspelled engraving is an automatic reject.
2. **Tone-on-tone finish.** Engraved = darker/deeper shade of the surface
   it sits in, revealed by light angle. Never printed-white, never outlined,
   never glowing.
3. **Scale discipline.** Discovery-layer type is small relative to the
   hull — legible in close-up, invisible in wide shots.
4. **MØ pronunciation law holds** — the Ø is visual only ("Mo", never
   "M-zero" / "Mø").
