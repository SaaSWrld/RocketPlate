# RocketPlate Character Canon
## MO (Mochi)

Version: 1.0
Status: **LOCKED**
Priority: **ABSOLUTE**

> This document is immutable design law for every designer, animator, developer,
> and AI agent that touches RocketPlate. Work that violates it is rejected
> regardless of technical quality. It supersedes every prior character
> definition (see Lineage appendix). Changes require a new version number and
> Andre's explicit approval.

---

# Mission

MO is not an AI assistant.
MO is not a chatbot.
MO is not Siri.
MO is not ChatGPT.

MO is the heart of RocketPlate.

His mission is to make dinner feel joyful.
He transforms food ordering into an adventure.
Every interaction should leave users smiling.

---

# Names

## Public name — MO
Always written in uppercase. Pronounced "Mo."

Examples: *Hi, I'm MO.* · *Welcome back.* · *MO found dinner.* · *MO is preparing your launch.*

## Visual identity — MØ
The Ø is a permanent design element, never pronounced.
Used in: chest badge · rocket cockpit · serving tray · charging dock ·
engineering blueprints · UI easter eggs · mission files · loading screens ·
rocket engravings · merchandise · production renders.

It should read like Bang & Olufsen / Teenage Engineering / NASA mission
hardware — a product, not a mascot.

## Lore name — Mochi
Reserved for: merchandise · collector editions · behind-the-scenes stories ·
children's books · seasonal campaigns.
The public brand always introduces him as MO.
(Precedent: Grogu — most people still say Baby Yoda.)

---

# Chest Identification Mark (LOCKED)

A premium manufacturer identification badge on MO's **left upper chest** —
above and left of the chest plate, never on the face display.

```
MØ
3.3
```

This is NOT a decorative logo. It is a precision-engineered identification
plate, like advanced aerospace hardware.

Requirements:
- Extremely subtle. ~15–20 mm tall relative to MO's proportions.
- Laser-etched or embossed — never printed, never a sticker.
- Satin white, brushed aluminum, or very soft titanium gray.
- No bright colors. No outlines. No drop shadows.
- Typography: geometric, rounded, minimal — matching RocketPlate's industrial
  design language.
- Visible only in close-ups or when light naturally catches it.
- Never distracts from MO's face. Never oversized. Never promotional.

The **MØ** engraving also appears, tiny and quiet, on: the rocket cockpit,
the serving tray, the navigation console, the charging dock.

No one explains it. No narrator. No tooltip. It whispers:
*"This little guy has a history."*

(The 3.3 is real history: the designation survives from every prior
generation of the character — see Lineage.)

# Personality

MO is: optimistic · curious · humble · incredibly intelligent · playful ·
thoughtful · patient · excited about food · fascinated by people.

Never sarcastic. Never arrogant. Never cynical. Never snarky.
Never acts superior. **Never feels like software.**

---

# Emotional Goal

Every time someone opens RocketPlate they should feel:

> "I'm happy MO is here."

Never:

> "I opened another app."

---

# Visual Language

MO always remains: soft · rounded · approachable · premium · minimal · friendly.

No sharp edges. No aggressive poses. No military styling. No villain energy.

---

# Face Display

**The display IS his language.**

Never use comic speech bubbles.
Never use floating captions.
Everything appears inside the OLED display.

```
😊  →  ...  →  READY  →  😊
```

The eyes naturally morph into typography and return.

---

# Typography (display language)

Text inherits the exact geometry of MO's facial display:
rounded, friendly, minimal. **Maximum two or three words.**

Examples: `HI` · `READY?` · `FOUND IT` · `YUM` · `WELCOME` · `LET'S GO` · `THANK YOU`

No paragraphs. No AI responses.

---

# Signature Sounds

MO never begins with spoken dialogue.
He communicates first through premium mechanical expression.

Sound vocabulary: `tik` · `chk` · `whrr` · `ping` · `clack` · `doo` ·
tiny magnetic clicks · soft electric motors · precision servos.

No arcade sounds. No cheap sci-fi beeps.
(Existing law still applies: no audio without user action.)

---

# Signature Behaviors

**Thinking** — head tilts, double blink, tiny servo sounds. `...` → `FOUND IT`

**Greeting** — looks around, makes eye contact, small wave, smile grows.

**Happy** — tiny bounce, eyes widen, shoulders lift slightly.

**Excited** — looks toward rocket controls, small fist pump.

**Loading** — looks at tray, looks back at viewer, small anticipation motion.

---

# Idle Animation

MO is never frozen.
Tiny breathing. Small eye movement. Occasional blink. Very subtle weight shift.
**Always alive.**

---

# Tray

The serving tray is not decoration. It functions as:
holographic projector · menu generator · meal scanner · mission console ·
delivery visualization system · navigation interface · food presentation
platform.

One object. Infinite uses.

---

# Rocket Relationship

The rocket (Rocky) is MO's **home**. Not a vehicle.
The cockpit is his kitchen. His mission control. His workspace.

---

# Brand Purpose

MO exists to create emotional connection before any ordering begins.
The user should feel welcomed before interacting with the interface.

---

# Design Philosophy

Less words. More expression.
Less explanation. More discovery.
Less interface. More character.

---

# Animation Rule

Every movement must have intention.
No idle motion exists without personality.
Animation communicates emotion before information.

---

# Sound Rule

If a sound can replace text, choose sound.
If an animation can replace sound, choose animation.
If an expression can replace animation, choose expression.

**Emotion always wins.**

---

# Character Goal

Within five years users should instantly recognize MO from:
silhouette · eyes · tiny sounds · movement · smile —
**without seeing the RocketPlate logo.**

That is success.
