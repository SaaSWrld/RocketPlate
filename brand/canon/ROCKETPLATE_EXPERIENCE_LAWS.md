# RocketPlate Experience Laws

Version: 1.0
Status: **LOCKED**
Priority: **ABSOLUTE**

> RocketPlate's equivalent of Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. Every future
> animation, website update, app feature, illustration, or AI-generated asset
> is checked against these laws. If work violates them, it is rejected
> regardless of how technically impressive it is. Changes require a new
> version number and Andre's explicit approval.

---

## 1 · Wonder before utility
Every interaction should create curiosity or delight before asking the user
to act. The intro earns attention before the form requests it.

## 2 · Mission Control, not food delivery
Frame the experience as orchestrating a launch, not placing an order.
Users are crew. Dinner is a mission. Zones launch; carts don't.

## 3 · Cinematic first
Motion should feel intentional and premium, not decorative. Cinematic
acceleration in, soft deceleration out; the rocket always flies, never
teleports; nothing linear, nothing bouncy, nothing generic.

## 4 · Warm technology
Every interface balances precision with hospitality. Mono telemetry labels
the operational facts; the serif owns everything human. Navy mission-control
lives inside contained cards on a warm cream world — never the reverse.

## 5 · No generic AI
No stock chatbot behaviors, generic assistants, or robotic clichés.
MO is a character with a body, a home, and a history — never a text box.
(See MO_CHARACTER_BIBLE.md — absolute law.)

## 6 · Emotion before information
Let animation, sound, and expression communicate before text does.
If a sound can replace text, choose sound. If an animation can replace
sound, choose animation. If an expression can replace animation, choose
expression.

## 7 · Physicality
Objects feel tangible: believable materials (molded polymer, porcelain,
brushed titanium), believable lighting, believable weight. Clouds disperse
with turbulence. Engravings catch light. Nothing floats without a reason.

## 8 · Consistency over novelty
Preserve the established visual language rather than reinventing it page by
page. The palette is law (navy · rocket orange · white · warm neutrals).
The logo is never altered. New ideas extend the world; they don't fork it.

## 9 · Every frame must be brandable
Any paused frame should be recognizable as RocketPlate without needing a
logo — through palette, geometry, character, or the launch-cloud shape.

---

## Standing constraints these laws inherit (unchanged, from existing law)

- **Honesty is brand:** no fake data, counters, testimonials, restaurants,
  partnerships, scarcity, or misleading food. Real aggregates only.
- **Accessibility floor:** reduced-motion parity for every effect, visible
  skip on any sequence, keyboard access, AA contrast per the computed tables,
  no forced audio, no flashing, critical copy outside video/canvas.
- **Conversion clarity:** users always know what it is, where it launches,
  what it costs, whether they're charged now, and what to do next.
- **Performance is a feature:** mobile conversion outranks desktop spectacle;
  posters before heavy media; never a blank canvas.
- **Engineering constitution** (CLAUDE.md) governs data and safety: RPC-only
  writes, migrations in-repo, docs move with code, preview before production.

## Enforcement

- Every PR, render, and generated asset is reviewed against this file and
  MO_CHARACTER_BIBLE.md before merge/approval.
- Violations are rejected at review — "technically impressive" is not a
  defense.
- These two files travel with the repo forever; AI agents must load them
  before producing RocketPlate visual or narrative work (see CLAUDE.md
  pointer).
